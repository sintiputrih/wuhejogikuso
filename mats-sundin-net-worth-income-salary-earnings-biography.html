Mats Sundin Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Mats Johan Sundin (born February 13, 1971; Swedish pronunciation: [mats s?ndi?n]) is a former Swedish professional ice hockey player. In 2009, he left the National Hockey League (NHL). Sundin spent his first four seasons in the NHL with the Quebec Nordiques, when he was chosen first overall in 1989. In 1994, he was sent to the Toronto Maple Leafs, where he spent the bulk of his career, including 11 seasons as captain. Sundin was the longest-serving non-North American born captain in NHL history at the conclusion of the 2007–08 season. Sundin announced his retirement on September 30, 2009, after playing for the Vancouver Canucks in the 2008–09 season. He spent 13 of his 18 NHL seasons with the  Toronto Maple Leafs in a row. In ten seasons, he participated in the NHL playoffs. He has the most games played for Canadian teams in his career. Sundin earned at least 70 points in every season of his career, save for his rookie season, the abbreviated lockout season, and his half-season with the Vancouver Canucks, and led the Leafs in points in every year he was with the club until 2002–03, when Alexander Mogilny beat him by seven points. Sundin became the first Swedish player to reach 500 goals on October 14, 2006. He is the all-time leader in goals (420) and points with the Leafs (984). Sundin averaged slightly over a point a game throughout his career (1349 points in 1346 NHL games). Sundin represented Sweden in three World Championship gold medals and a gold medal at the 2006 Winter Olympics in Turin. On June 26, 2012, Sundin was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame. It was his first year as a student. Following Börje Salming (another long-time Maple Leafs player in his own NHL career), he became the second Swede to be inducted into the Hall of Fame. In 2013, he was inducted into the IIHF Hall of Fame.
