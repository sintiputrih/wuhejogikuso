What is Brian Grazer’s Net Worth?

Brian Grazer, a film and television producer, has a net worth of $400 million. He is best known for co-founding Imagine Entertainment with Ron Howard, through which he has produced many award-winning films, including “Apollo 13,” “A Beautiful Mind,” and “Frost/Nixon.” Grazer has also produced popular television shows such as “Felicity,” “Arrested Development,” “Friday Night Lights,” and “Empire.”

Brian Grazer’s Background

Brian Grazer was born in Los Angeles in 1951 to Arlene and Thomas. His parents divorced when he was in high school, and he was raised by his grandmother. He attended the University of Southern California on a scholarship, graduating from the film school in 1974. He briefly attended USC Law School but left in 1975 to pursue his career in Hollywood.

Grazer has two younger siblings named Nora and Gavin.

Career Highlights of Brian Grazer

Brian Grazer started his career producing television movies like “Zuma Beach” and “Thou Shalt Not Commit Adultery” in the late 70s. He then went on to executive-produce several television pilots at Paramount Pictures in the early 80s, where he met his future business partner Ron Howard. Grazer produced his first feature film, Howard’s “Night Shift,” in 1982, and two years later, the duo reunited for the fantasy romance “Splash,” which earned him an Academy Award nomination for Best Original Screenplay.

In 1985, Grazer and Howard co-founded Imagine Entertainment, which produced several successful films like “Like Father Like Son,” “Parenthood,” “Kindergarten Cop,” “My Girl,” “Backdraft,” “Far and Away,” “Housesitter,” “Boomerang,” “For Love or Money,” “My Girl 2,” “Greedy,” “The Paper,” and “The Cowboy Way.” However, Grazer’s greatest triumph came in 1995, when he produced Howard’s historical space film “Apollo 13,” which won him the Daryl F. Zanuck Motion Picture Producer of the Year Award from the Producers Guild of America and an Academy Award nomination for Best Picture.

Brian Grazer’s Career Highlights

Brian Grazer produced several successful films such as “Sgt. Bilko,” “Fear,” “The Nutty Professor,” “The Chamber,” “Ransom,” “Liar Liar,” “Inventing the Abbotts,” “Mercury Rising,” “EDtv,” and “Bowfinger” after the success of “Apollo 13.”

In the 2000s, he produced “Nutty Professor II: The Klumps,” “How the Grinch Stole Christmas,” “A Beautiful Mind,” “Undercover Brother,” “Blue Crush,” and “8 Mile” among others. He received his fourth Academy Award nomination for producing “Frost/Nixon” in 2008.

His producing credits in the 2010s include “Robin Hood,” “The Dilemma,” “Restless,” “Cowboys & Aliens,” “Tower Heist,” “J. Edgar,” “Rush,” “Get on Up,” “In the Heart of the Sea,” “Inferno,” “The Spy Who Dumped Me,” and “Dads.” He produced Ron Howard’s “Rebuilding Paradise” and “Hillbilly Elegy” in 2020 and the film adaptation of the musical “tick, tick… BOOM!” in the next year.

Brian Grazer’s Prolific Television Career

Brian Grazer has had a long and successful career in television, serving as executive producer on numerous shows throughout the 80s, 90s, 2000s, and 2010s. Some of his early credits include “Shadow Chasers,” “Ohara,” and “Smart Guys,” as well as the television movies “Poison” and “Mutts.” In the 90s, he worked on “Hiller and Diller,” “Felicity,” and “From the Earth to the Moon.”

In the 2000s, Grazer was executive producer on shows such as “The Beast,” “Miss Match,” “Arrested Development,” and “The Big House,” as well as television movies like “B.S.,” “The Snobs,” and “The Break.” He continued to work in television throughout the 2010s, producing shows like “Parenthood,” “The Great Escape,” “24: Live Another Day,” and “Genius.”

Grazer’s impressive list of credits also includes “Shark,” “Friday Night Lights,” “Lie to Me,” “Empire,” and “Wu-Tang: An American Saga.” With such a prolific career in television, it’s no wonder that Grazer has amassed a net worth of over $400 million.

Brian Grazer’s Marriages

Brian Grazer has been married multiple times. His first wife was Corki Corman, whom he wed in 1982. The pair had two children named Riley and Sage, and divorced in 1992.

In 1997, Grazer married screenwriter and novelist Gigi Levangie, with whom he had two sons named Thomas and Patrick. The couple divorced in 2007.

Grazer subsequently got engaged to Veronica Smiley, the chief marketing officer at a hotel management company; they married in 2016. Grazer resides in Santa Monica, California, and also has a home on Sunset Beach in Hawaii.

Brian Grazer’s Real Estate Transactions

In 2003, Brian Grazer purchased a home in the Malibu Colony for $13.5 million. After his divorce from Gigi Levangie, he listed the property for $16 million in 2007 and again for $19.5 million in 2011. The house ultimately sold for $17.4 million in February 2014.

Grazer and Levangie also put their Pacific Palisades home on the market in 2007 for $27.5 million. It sold for $17.55 million in March 2009 to Ben Affleck and Jennifer Garner. In January 2019, Affleck and Garner sold the home to Adam Levine for $32 million.

In December 2011, Grazer purchased a 10,000 square-foot home in Santa Monica for $12.5 million. The property had previously been listed for $22.5 million three years prior.
