J P Arencibia

J P Arencibia is a well-known former professional baseball catcher who now works as a studio commentator for Bally Sports Florida. He was a member of the Toronto Blue Jays, Texas Rangers, and Tampa Bay Rays in Major League Baseball from 2010 to 2015.

Jonathan Paul Arencibia was born in Miami, Florida on January 5, 1986. He is a United States citizen with a Cuban-American ethnicity.

Education Background

He went to Westminster Christian High School in Miami, Florida for his high school education. In high school, he also participated in sports such as football and basketball, but baseball was his favorite. Later, he was a member of the Florida Bombers’ Under-18 team.

He then went on to play college baseball at the University of Tennessee, where he won prizes such as the USA Baseball Richard W “Dick” Case Player of the Year in 2006. The Blue Jays decided to keep him at catcher rather than shift him to first base since he was a solid defensive catcher. In Cuba, he also played baseball.

Career and Professional Life

In the 2007 MLB draft, he was chosen 21st overall by this club. He began the 2008 season with the Dunedin Blue Jays of the Florida State League. Before the all-star break, he led the FSL in a number of offensive categories, hitting 315 with 13 home runs, 22 doubles, and 62 RBI in the first half of the season.

He was promoted to the Blue Jays’ Double-A affiliate, the New Hampshire Fisher Cats of the Eastern League, after this game. He participated in the Arizona Fall League for the Phoenix Desert Dogs during the 2008 offseason. For the 2009 season, he was promoted to the Blue Jays’ Triple-A affiliate in the Pacific Coast League, the Las Vegas 51s. He was promoted to MLB in 2010 to replace John Buck, who was injured.

He made his Major League Baseball debut in 2010 for the Toronto Blue Jays against the Tampa Bay Rays. He then returned to Las Vegas on September 1. As a result, he was optioned back to Triple-A to make room for John Buck’s return.

He debuted with the Blue Jays as a catcher in the 2011 home opening, leading and winning multiple games against the Kansas City Royals, Tampa Bay Rays, and Boston Red Sox. Then, in 2012, he played the longest game in Blue Jays history in terms of innings on opening day. He was the first catcher to receive the award in Blue Jays history. On December 2, 2013, he became a free agent for the first time in his career.

He signed a minor-league agreement with the Baltimore Orioles on January 8, 2015, with an invitation to spring training, and was released on April 9. On April 16, Arencibia signed a minor-league contract with the Tampa Bay Rays before being released on August 26. On December 14, 2015, he stated that he had signed a minor league contract with the Philadelphia Phillies, which he later cancelled on May 16, 2016.

He returned on May 20, 2016, to sign a minor league contract with the Tampa Bay Rays. On January 18, 2017, he announced his retirement from professional baseball. In 2019, he began working as a studio before and after game analyst for Fox Sports Florida’s Marlins broadcasts.
