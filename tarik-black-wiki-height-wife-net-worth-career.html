How did Tarik Black start his Professional Career?

  • Ridgeway High School in Memphis, Tennessee was where Black went to school. As a junior in 2008-09, he averaged 15.7 points and 11.8 rebounds per game, leading the Roadrunners to a 26-6 record and the District 15-AAA championship. As a senior in 2009-10, he led the Roadrunners to a 26-3 record and their second District 15-AAA championship, averaging 16.3 points and 12.6 rebounds.
  • In his debut season at Memphis, Black averaged 9.1 points and 5.0 rebounds per game, appearing in all 35 games and starting 24, including 18 of the last 19. Following that, he was named to the Conference USA All-Freshman team.
  • In his second season, Black averaged 10.7 points, 4.9 rebounds, and 1.5 blocks per game while playing in all 35 games and starting 31 of them. He was also named to the All-Conference USA second team, as well as the All-Defensive and All-Tournament teams. He also set a single-season Memphis field goal percentage record of 68.9 percent.
  • During his junior season, Black appeared in 32 games and made five starts, averaging 8.1 points and 4.8 rebounds in 20.8 minutes per game. He was named to the Tiger Academic 30 for the 2013 spring semester because he had the highest grade-point average on the team.
  • On May 20, 2013, Black graduated from Memphis University with a degree in organizational leadership, relocated to the University of Kansas, and was immediately authorized to play. In his lone season with the Jayhawks, he was named the Big 12 Preseason Newcomer of the Year. In the NCAA Tournament, he averaged 15.0 points per game.
  • He produced a season-high 19 points on 9-of-9 shooting on Senior Night, the best efficiency by a Jayhawk since 1990. In 33 games (15 starts), he averaged 5.5 points and 3.9 rebounds in 13.5 minutes per game.
    After going undrafted in the 2014 NBA draft, Black joined the Houston Rockets for the 2014 NBA Summer League. He signed with the Rockets on August 27, 2014. He appeared in 25 games for the Rockets until being released on December 26, 2014.
  • On December 28, the Los Angeles Lakers claimed Black off waivers. On January 3, he was assigned to the NBA Development League’s Los Angeles D-Fenders.
  • He had a season-best with 18 points and 10 rebounds against the Minnesota Timberwolves on April 10, 2015. He was called up the next day.
  • During the 2015-16 season, Black was assigned to the Los Angeles D-Fenders on many occasions.
    On August 24, 2016, Black re-signed with the Lakers. The Lakers released Black on July 1, 2017.
