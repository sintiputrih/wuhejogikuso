Biography

Neil Diamond was born in Brooklyn, NY on January 24, 1941. One of the most successful contemporary artists in the history of the Billboard chart, he has sold over 125 million records and is known for hits like “Kentucky Woman,” “Sweet Caroline,” and “America.” He and Barbra Streisand were classmates at Erasmus Hall High School and they would later sing a duet on Diamond’s song “You Don’t Bring Me Flowers,” which would become a #1 hit. He won an NCAA National Championship as part of the New York University fencing team. During his final year at NYU, he was offered a gig writing music for a publishing company.
