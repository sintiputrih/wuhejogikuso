How did Yankees get Lucas Luetge? Where did Lucas Luetge go to college?

Lucas Luetge is an American professional baseball pitcher for the New York Yankees of Major League Baseball (MLB).

Lucas Lester Luetge was born on March 24, 1987, in Brenham, Texas, the United States. He was born to Rocky Luetge and his wife Melissa Luetge.

Lucas Luetge attended Bellville High School in Bellville, Texas and Rice University, where he played college baseball for the Rice Owls baseball team. The Milwaukee Brewers drafted Luetge in the 21st round of the 2008 MLB draft. He was later selected by the Seattle Mariners from the Brewers on December 8, 2011, in the Rule 5 Draft.

Lucas Luetge had previously played in MLB for the Seattle Mariners and made his MLB debut on April 7, 2012. He signed a minor league contract with the New York Yankees for the 2021 season.

Lucas Luetge is happily married to his beloved wife, Lacie Luetge. He and his partner have welcomed two sons and one daughter including Eva, Jackson and Gavin Luetge.

How did Yankees get Lucas Luetge?

Free-agent, Lucas Luetge signed a minor league contract with the New York Yankees for the 2021 season who also gave him a non-roster invitation to spring training.

Where did Lucas Luetge go to college?

Lucas Luetge went to college at Rice University and San Jacinto College: Central Campus.
