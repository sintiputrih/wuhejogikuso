Biography

Fay McKenzie was born in California on February 19, 1918. Most well known for her performances in the 1940s Gene Autry films Down Mexico Way, Sierra Sue, Cowboy Serenade, Heart of the Rio Grande, and Home in Wyomin.’ She is also notable for her supporting role in the 1968 Peter Sellers film The Party. She performed with Bert Lahr (who is most well known for his portrayal of the Cowardly Lion in the classic film The Wizard of Oz) in a Broadway production of Burlesque. She appeared in her first film, Station Content (1918), when she was less than three months old and went on to make childhood appearances in several other silent movies.
