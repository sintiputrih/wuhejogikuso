Where is Pandora in ‘Avatar’?

The potato-shaped moon Pandora is covered in a fine ice substance. In stark contrast to the sharply defined craters of other moons, including Hyperion, the craters on Pandora are even covered in garbage. The small moon’s surface also seems to be characterized by intriguing ridges and grooves.

The closest star system to the Solar System of humans is the Alpha Centauri System, home to the livable extrasolar moon Pandora. It is the gas giant Polyphemus’ fifth moon, which orbits Alpha Centauri A. Humans have given both the moon’s and its mother planet’s names in honor of characters from Greek mythology.

Despite being almost as big as Earth, Pandora is merely one of the thirteen moons that the gas giant Polyphemus has. However, because it is smaller than Earth, its gravity is 20% lower.

How Far Away Is Pandora?

Pandora is portrayed as a planet that is, apparently, far away in space in the world of Avatar. It was never specifically mentioned in Avatar (2009) film, where Pandora was or how long it would take humans to get there, though, because of the rich plot of Avatar and the numerous aspects of Pandora that were explored.

According to estimates, Pandora is 4.37 light-years far from our planet. It would still take longer than four years to get there even if you traveled at the speed of light.

Actually, Pandora is a moon rather than a planet. There are only 14 moons in total orbiting the gas giant planet Polyphemus, and due to its smaller size, Pandora has less gravity than the others. The Na’vi people live on the planet Pandora.

Is Pandora from Avatar a real place?

The film takes place on the fictional planet of Pandora, one of the numerous moons of the hypothetical Saturn-sized gas giant Polyphemus, which is situated in the actual Alpha Centauri system, the nearest star system to Earth at a distance of roughly 4.4 light-years.

How long did it take to travel to Pandora in Avatar?

It takes almost six years (five years, nine months, and 22 days) for JakeSully to reach Pandora in 2154, Avatar (2009) film.
