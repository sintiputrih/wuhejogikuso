Brian Walsh Cause Of Death: Brian Walsh was an Australian TV pioneer and an influential media executive.

He began his career with ABC before moving into feature film production and distribution at Palm Beach Pictures, and promotions and publicity at Sydney radio station 2SM.

Brian Walsh was known for his multi-faceted media career, including as a gifted media, entertainment, and sports publicist, and his important role in many iconic Australian television moments.

He was credited with the global success of “Neighbours”, including launching the careers of its actors Kylie Minogue, Jason Donocan, and Guy Pearce.

Walsh was also the driving force behind various Australian television shows including Neighbours, A Place to Call Home, Wentworth, and Colin from Accounts.

He was also responsible for the Australian Rugby League’s iconic ‘Simply The Best’ marketing campaign featuring singer Tina Turner in the 1990s.

During his extensive career, spanning almost five decades, Walsh held senior positions at the Ten Network, the UK’s Sky Broadcasting, Sky TV in Asia, and Australia’s Foxtel.

When he later joined the Ten Network, he was instrumental in the network’s decision in 1985 to acquire Neighbours after it had been dumped by rival channel Seven.

In 1995, Walsh joined Foxtel where he helped establish subscription television in Australia.

Most recently he worked as an executive at the company, Foxtel overseeing the development of original dramas.

Over the past two decades, he’s been overseeing scripted and unscripted commissions for the Foxtel Group, including the likes of “Wentworth”, “Upright”, “Love Me”, “Colin from Accounts” and “The Twelve”.
