Most Common Types of Magnesium Supplements

There are several types of magnesium you’ll find in magnesium supplements, with some types linked to supporting specific health issues. Let’s take a look at the most common forms of magnesium supplements and their uses.

Magnesium citrate

One of the most common magnesium supplements, magnesium citrate is a form of magnesium that’s bound with citric acid. It’s naturally found in citrus fruits, with the acid being responsible for the tarte flavor.

It’s believed to be one of the most bioavailable forms – i.e. most easily absorbable – forms of magnesium. Its common use is as a calm agent to help manage the symptoms of anxiety and depression. Magnesium citrate also has a natural laxative effect, so it’s sometimes used at higher doses to treat constipation.

Magnesium oxide

A salt that combines magnesium and oxygen, magnesium oxide is a white powdery substance often sold in powder or capsule form.

As this form of magnesium is poorly absorbed by the digestive tract, it is usually used to treat uncomfortable digestive issues, such as indigestion, heartburn and constipation. Magnesium oxide is also sometimes used to prevent and treat migraines.

Magnesium glycinate

Magnesium is formed from elemental magnesium and the amino acid glycine. This particular amino acid plays an important role in protein construction.

Magnesium glycinate is easily absorbed and, although more research is needed, it’s believed to have calming properties. Therefore, it may help reduce the symptoms of several mental health issues including depression, anxiety, stress and insomnia.

Magnesium chloride

Magnesium chloride is a salt that combines magnesium and chlorine. It is well absorbed in the digestive tract, which makes it a great all-around magnesium supplement. It can also be used to boost magnesium levels in those who are deficient in magnesium.

Magnesium chloride is most frequently found in capsules or tablets, but it can also be found in topicals, designed to help soothe everyday aches and pains.

Potential Benefits of Magnesium Supplements

Magnesium is involved in more than 300 essential metabolic reactions, including nerve signal transmission, muscle contraction, energy production, blood pressure regulation and so much more.

Although magnesium is found in high levels in many whole foods such as legumes, seeds, nuts and green leafy vegetables, it’s estimated as many as two-thirds of people in the Western world are falling short of their magnesium needs through diet alone.

Low levels of magnesium may be involved in various health and wellness concerns, such as osteoporosis, migraines, heart disease and more. Therefore, for those who don’t get enough of this vital mineral in their diet, magnesium supplements can have several potential health benefits.

Bone health

Around 50% to 60% of the body’s magnesium is found within the bones, being responsible for maintaining bone health and protecting against bone loss.

A recent review study found an association between high magnesium intake and increased bone density in bones that are susceptible to fracture. Further research demonstrates higher magnesium intake reduces the risk of bone loss, osteoporosis and fractures.

Heart health

Magnesium is essential in supporting heart health. A 2021 review study found that magnesium supplements can help lower high blood pressure, which is a risk factor for heart disease. Another review study found a link between high magnesium intake and a lower risk of stroke, high blood pressure and heart disease.

Further research has found that magnesium supplements improve multiple heart disease risk factors including reduced levels of LDL (bad cholesterol), higher levels of HDL (good) cholesterol and lowered blood pressure, particularly in those with a magnesium deficiency.

Boost exercise performance

Magnesium aids the transport of blood sugar into the muscles and the removal of lactate, which can build up during exercise causing muscle fatigue.

Research has shown that magnesium supplements can help boost exercise performance, particularly in those who are deficient in the mineral. Plus, magnesium can help to boost muscle power and mass, aid recovery and protect against muscle damage.

Anxiety symptoms

While current research is limited, early evidence suggests that magnesium supplements have the potential as a treatment for managing anxiety symptoms.

Furthermore, magnesium plays a key role in brain function and mood, with low levels linked to an increased risk of depression. Studies indicate that supplementing with magnesium can help reduce the symptoms of depression.

People Also Ask

Who can benefit from magnesium supplements?

Magnesium plays numerous key roles within the body, therefore, magnesium supplements can benefit anyone who isn’t getting enough of the vital mineral through their diet.

That being said, magnesium supplementation may not be suitable for those with diabetes, heart disease, kidney disease, or intestinal disease. If you have any pre-existing medical conditions check with your healthcare provider before taking a magnesium supplement.

How do magnesium supplements work?

Magnesium regulates the function of hundreds of enzymes, helps the body build essential proteins and acts as an important electrolyte. Through these actions, magnesium helps to manage many essential processes within the body, including amongst others:

  • Bone health
  • Energy production
  • Nerve signaling
  • Muscle function
  • Regulate blood pressure

How long does it take to see results?

With consistent daily supplementation, you may notice improvements in as little after a week of taking magnesium. However, it can take up to four weeks of consistent supplementation to see noticeable results, particularly if you’re treating a deficiency or looking to improve a condition.

Are magnesium supplements safe?

When taken by mouth, magnesium supplements are likely safe for most people, when following dosage instructions. Doses less than 350mg a day are typically considered safe for the majority of adults.

Can I take magnesium supplements with existing medication?

Magnesium from supplements can interact with some types of antibiotics and other medications, like those to prevent bone loss, certain heart medications and magnesium-containing antacids or laxatives. Therefore, you should check with your doctor or pharmacist if you’re considering adding a magnesium supplement to your daily routine.

Are there any side effects of magnesium supplements?

While magnesium is typically well tolerated, there are some potential side effects, which include nausea, cramps, softening of stool and diarrhea.

Too much magnesium can cause magnesium toxicity, which has more significant side effects including low blood pressure, fatigue, irregular breathing, urinary retention, vomiting and muscle weakness. A magnesium overdose – although rare – can be fatal. Therefore, when taking a magnesium supplement, stick with the manufacturers’ dosage instructions.
